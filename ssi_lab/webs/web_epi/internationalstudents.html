The Polytechnic School of Engineering of Gijón is a national benchmark centre. Its origin goes back to the the end of the nineteenth century when the School of Arts and Crafts in Gijón was established. After passing through several stages and by mergering the School of Industrial Engineering, the School of Computer Engineering and the School of Telecommunication Engineering, Gijón Polytechnic School of Engineering was established in 2010.

The School is located on the Campus of Gijón, this is the most populous city in Asturias. Gijón offers a wide and dynamic range of social-cultural events.

The School offers eight bachelor's degrees and serveral master's degrees in engineering. Four Departments of the University of Oviedo are headquartered and laboratories of near 30 Knowledge Areas of the University are located in the campus allowing to follow the doctoral in the same Campus in many different disciplines.

The School has modern facilities, thousands of square meters of landscape areas and some sports facilities. There are around 40 computer rooms which totals more than 800 computers available to students. We also have a library, canteens, two Main Halls and large areas of free-access parking.
